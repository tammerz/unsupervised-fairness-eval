Implications for Algorithmic Governance

1
Global Metrics Lie
Relying on absolute error parity or county-wide averages obscures severe directional harm. An AVM can mathematically satisfy fairness on paper while actively overcharging a low-income area and undercharging a neighboring one.
2
Proxies are Viable Auditing Tools
Unsupervised spatial clustering (C4F) successfully detects bias without requiring legally protected attributes. We can audit the black box from the outside.
3
Post-Hoc Supervision Works
Regulators do not need invasive white-box access to proprietary neural weights. By enforcing dimensional parity between spatial coordinates and socioeconomic ranks, we can map algorithmic blindspots post-deployment.
